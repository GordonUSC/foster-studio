A note to myself

Forty years ago I moved across the country to help Gordon build a career. All that time, without quite noticing, I was also collecting something for myself, one sound at a time.

A baby stroller folded in one frame. A Trapper Keeper in the pink alcove. Jazzology three feet from the Moog Muse. The abstract canvas hung above that Muse, all pale blue and orange stars and white swirl, that says more about me than any faceplate in the room. A hot-pink Bébé Chérie on an overturned milk crate. A Tektronix scope on the desk, because I do not just buy the sound, I open it up and make it mine. None of this is gear. Each piece is a night, a chord, a room, a feeling I wanted to hold onto. The books and the synths are the same object to me: an experience I keep coming back to.

So this is not a studio. It is a museum of my own ear. I am not collecting gear. I am collecting myself, and every piece is a moment I refuse to let go of. Nothing here was a trade-off. Every one of these was a yes. Now I get to open it up and play it.

Now I go play it. Fang Speare · the toad-sage
